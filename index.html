Ajokon Goat Empowerment Project

Empowering communities through sustainable livestock rearing to eradicate generational poverty in Amuria, Uganda.

Explore Our Model Meet the Team

Addressing Poverty Across Three Dimensions

AGEP transcends traditional charity by fostering a self-sustaining community micro-enterprise ecosystem.

Economic

Establishing household financial resilience via appreciating livestock assets.

Social

Fostering collective responsibility and mutual trust via peer accountability mechanisms.

Environmental

Advancing sustainable practices through organic manure utilization and drought-resistant livestock farming.

The Sustainable Replication Model

Understanding the 'Pass-on-the-Gift' System

1

Beneficiary families receive two goats complemented by comprehensive training on livestock care.

2

Upon reproduction, families retain the offspring while transferring the parent goats to another beneficiary family.

3

The Goat Bank serves as a communal insurance mechanism for goat replacements.

Implementation

Step-by-step process of the AGEP project implementation.

1. Identification of beneficiaries and vetting them according to criteria

2. Recruitment and alignment meeting

3. Goats bought treated and tagged

4. Beneficiaries gathered for vet training

5. Goats distributed to upto 20 beneficiaries

6. AGEP savings sacco formed headed by the beneficiaries head committee

7. Project monitoring team for visits chosen amongst the beneficiaries themselves
